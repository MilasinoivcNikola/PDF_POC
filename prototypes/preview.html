Cover & Dedication

Saying Goodbye to

Otis

A story for Emma

For Emma,
and for Otis,
who loved her so very much.

2014 — 2026

i
Pages 2 & 3

Once, in a home full of love, there lived a dog named Otis.

Otis was a sweet rescue mutt, possibly part beagle, with floppy ears and the softest brown eyes — eyes that always knew when you needed a friend.

02

Otis loved Emma more than anything in the whole world.

And Emma loved Otis right back.

03
Pages 6 & 7 · The gentle pages

There was one day Emma will always remember.

The day Otis followed her to the lake, and they both came home soaking wet, laughing the whole way.

That was the kind of love Otis and Emma shared.

06

But every living thing has a beginning, a middle, and an end. Even the ones we love most.

Otis's body got tired.

And then, very gently, his body stopped working. That is what "died" means.

07
Pages 9 & 10 · Where love goes

Some people say there is a place called the Rainbow Bridge.

A sunny meadow where pets run free, where bodies don't get tired anymore.

Where Otis can chase tennis balls in the backyard for as long as he wants.

09

Here is the most important thing of all.

The love between Emma and Otis did not stop when Otis died.

Love does not work that way.

It stays.
It always stays.

10
Pages 11 & 12 · The closing

When Emma misses Otis — and that will happen many times — there are things Emma can do.

Look at a picture. Tell a story. Say Otis's name out loud — names are meant to be said.

11

Otis was a good boy.
Otis was Emma's dog.

And Otis will always,
always be loved.

— end —
