Iteration 001 proposed splitting the storefront into **two worlds**: *celebrate
them* (4 joyful/living titles) vs *remember them* (4 memorial/tribute titles),
used on both the landing (two `.world` panels) and `/books` (two labelled sections,
`#joyful` / `#memorial` anchors). Accent mapping proposed: **joyful = gold**,
**memorial = rose**.

The four-and-four grouping (as of 2026-06-15):
- Joyful: Story 8 Adventures, Story 7 Welcome Home, Story 4 If…Could Talk, Story 9 New Baby.
- Memorial/tribute: Story 1 Saying Goodbye, Story 2 Letter from, Story 5 Letter to,
  Story 6 While You're Still Here (a *living* tribute but grouped with goodbye — borderline).

**Why:** the catalog grew to 8 titles, ~half grief / half celebration. The live
landing dumps all 8 chooser cards in one stack and `/books` lists all 8 in one flat
grid — a grieving visitor wades through adventure books and vice versa. The live
landing already gives Stories 6/7/8/9 bespoke per-card accents; the two-worlds idea
collapses those into two families.

**How to apply:** this is a PROPOSAL, not an accepted baseline — do not treat the
split as shipped. The open questions in 001/NOTES.md (is the split the primary
catalog nav? is gold/rose the right mapping? where does Story 6 belong? drop the
Story-1 TOC from the landing?) are unresolved. When the PM rules, record the verdict
here and which iteration became the accepted baseline. See [[design-system]].
